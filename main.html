Lost World of Tambun

The Lost World of Tambun in Ipoh, Perak, is a premier eco-adventure destination offering a unique blend of natural beauty, thrilling attractions, and cultural experiences. Surrounded by lush rainforests and limestone cliffs, it features a water park, amusement rides, a petting zoo, and a Tiger Valley. Visitors can unwind in natural hot springs, explore the enchanting Luminous Forest, and enjoy cultural performances that celebrate Malaysia's heritage. With activities like zip-lining, rock climbing, and glamping, along with a vibrant night park experience, the Lost World of Tambun promises unforgettable fun for families and adventure seekers alike.

Kellie's Castle

Kellie’s Castle, located near Batu Gajah in Perak, Malaysia, is a historic and mysterious landmark known for its unique architecture and intriguing history. Built by Scottish planter William Kellie Smith in the early 20th century, the castle was intended to be a luxurious mansion blending Moorish, Indian, and Scottish architectural styles. The construction was abandoned after Smith’s sudden death, leaving the castle incomplete and surrounded by mystery and tales of hauntings. Visitors can explore its grand halls, hidden passageways, and rooftop offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Kellie’s Castle is a fascinating destination for history enthusiasts and those intrigued by its eerie charm and romantic backstory.
